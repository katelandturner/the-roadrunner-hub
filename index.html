The problem
Amazon's review process is costing time, money and momentum
The solution
RoadRunner — an AI-powered creative review and production platform
This is...
✕Not a SaaS product
✓Proprietary tooling built around Amazon's enterprise environment
✓Comes from embedded producers and our creative talent network
✓Comes with the team, not instead of it
Benefits to the client
🎯Reviewers motivated to engage — time-boxed, guided, fewer contradictions
📊Measurable AI efficiency — built in, not bolted on. Answers Amazon's internal mandate directly
🔁Feedback compounds — same note never gets made twice, noise reduces every round
⚙️Production focused — routine changes handled by AI, complex ones still queue to humans
📦Brand knowledge built in — brand × territory × language × project layer
✏️Reviewers feel empowered — fix small things themselves, within guardrails. No one else has this.
Benefits to Multiplayer
📈The market is moving. Agencies rebuilding around AI are delivering faster, at quality, without scaling headcount — this is what clients will expect as standard
🏗Proves we can deliver at market speed, at quality, using AI — our ability to compete depends on it
🤝Strengthens Creative N—S embedded model — the closer we are to how a client actually works, the better placed we are to remove friction
This is also a Multiplayer business investment
The methodology — Claude Code in the middle, building fast, hardening, shipping, repeating — is intended to become a repeatable model for how Multiplayer builds and delivers. RoadRunner is the proof of concept for that model as much as it is a product in its own right.
The methodology — and why it's not an afterthought
This project runs on connected infrastructure: ClickUp (Dev), Notion (Production), and Slack (#mp_roadrunner) all feed into a single CoWork dashboard that updates daily. Every workstream speaks to the others. The result is a complete, accurate picture of where we are in the plan — without anyone having to chase it.

This matters because moving fast only works if the team stays aligned without burning time on it. When everything is tracked and visible, we spend less time in meetings asking "where are we?" and more time on the conversations that actually need the room. Blockers surface early, friction between workstreams reduces, and a shared source of truth replaces email chains, repeated check-ins, and guesswork. This is the infrastructure that makes pace sustainable — and it only works if everyone commits to keeping it current.
